Aguachile

    Ingredients

  • 1 pound raw shrimp (peeled and deveined, tails removed)
  • 1–2 large limes
  • salt
  • ¼ of a red onion, sliced on a mandolin
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 bunch cilantro
  • 2 serano chili, partially deseeded
  • 1 1/2 cups sliced English cucumber
  • avocado slices
  • Juanita's tortilla chips

    Directions

  1. Butterfly the shrimp and spread in a shallow servign dish. Squeeze with enough lime to cover the shrimp and sprinkle with a little salt. Turn shrimp over as needed to denature both sides in the lime juice – this will take about 20 minutes.
  2. Thinly slice the red onion, place it in a bowl and season generously with salt. Pour just enough water to cover the onions.
  3. Make the Aguachile Marinade: Place the garlic, remaining lime juice, serranos, and cilantro (reserving some cilantro for garnish) in a blender and blend until smooth.
  4. Pour the aguachile marinade over the shrimp and toss to coat.
  5. Drain the onions and scatter them over top, mixing them in slightly. Refrigerate 30 minutes or up to 4 hours.
  6. When ready to serve, taste and adjust salt. Add cucumber and avocado slices to the top. Scatter with fresh cilantro and serve with tortilla chips
