Weeknight Ramen

Ingredients

  • 1 rotisserie chicken
  • 4 scallions
  • 1" piece fresh ginger root, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, cut into thick slices
  • 1 drid shitake mushroom or 1 Tbs umami powder
  • 3 slice bacon
  • 1 quart water
  • 1 Tbs miso
  • soy sauce and salt to taste
  • 3-4 packages top ramen (or fresh ramen noodles if you can faind them)
  • Soy Marinated Eggs
  • corn niblits
  • bean sprouts
  • furikake

Directions

  1. Remove the drumsticks and breast meat from the chicken. Shred the leg and breast meat but leave plenty of chicken on the carcass. Reserve the shredded meat in the fridge.
  2. Break the chicken carcass into a few pieces and put them in a stockpot. Trim the roots dark green parts of the scallions and add them to the pot; thinly slice the white and tender green parts and reserve them to garnish the soup. Add the ginger, carrot, shiitake, and bacon. Add the water and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat from a rolling boil to a rollicking simmer, and skim any froth that collects on the surface during the first 10 minutes of simmering.
  3. Cook until the liquid is reduced by one-third (to 8 cups), about 2 hours. Strain the broth, reserving the shittake to slice and use as garnish. Stir in miso and season to taste
  4. While you bring the broth back to a strong simmer, cook the ramen noodles according to package directions. Drain the noodles very well, and portion them out among 4 deep soup bowls. Top with reserved chicken meat, corn, and a split soy egg. Pour broth around and garnish with reserved scallion and furikake.
