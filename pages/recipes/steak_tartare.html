Steak Tartare

    Ingredients

  • 6oz very fresh, well trimmed beef tenderloin
  • 1 egg yolk, fresh or cured
  • 1 tsp whole grain or dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp olive oil + more for brushing bread
  • 1/2 tsp worcestershie
  • 1/2 tsp tobassco
  • 1 tsp onion, finest dice and soaked in cold water 10 minutes
  • 1 tsp capers, minced
  • 1 tsp cornichon, minced
  • 1 tsp parsely, minced
  • 1 baguette

    Directions

  1. Place the tenderloin in the freezer for 10 minutes. Then, With your sharpest knife, dice the tenderloin as small as possible.
  2. Combine the beef, olive oil, worcestershie, mustard, tobassco, onion, caper, cornichon, and parsley in a bowl. Sprinkle with salt, mix gently, and taste for seasoning. The seasoning step is really imporant since I just made up these measurements and always make this recipe by look and taste.
  3. Slice the baguette thinly on a slight bias and brush each slice with olive oil. Spread the slices on a sheet pan and toast in the broiler.
  4. Mound the tartare in the center of a serving plate and surround with toasts
  5. If using a fresh egg yolk, make a slight depression in the top of the mound with the back of a spoon. Place the fresh yolk in the depression and sprinkle with finishin salt. If using cured yolk, use a microplane to finely grate the yolk over the tartare.
